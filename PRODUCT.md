# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors to The_Lonely_Devil's BotBooru creator profile, especially people deciding whether to browse, follow, request, or use the creator's character cards. They arrive inside BotBooru's existing UI, often scanning quickly on desktop or mobile, and need the profile to communicate taste, craft, trust, and personality without making the page hard to read. Many arrive as Chub-era followers checking where the creator went.

## Product Purpose

The profile is a paste-deployable bio surface for a BotBooru creator. It should frame the creator as meticulous, opinionated, and active while guiding visitors toward the cards, requests, recommendations, and credits. Success means the page feels memorable and personal, but also clean enough that the profile content and card links are easy to scan.

## Brand Personality

Clean, premium, ember-orange on deep brown, warm. The creator identity should read immediately as amber/orange and black, with Firefly's teal used as a secondary accent for headings, bullets, and detail contrast. The voice can stay blunt and playful as long as the visual system feels intentional.

## Anti-references

Avoid muddy brown/orange, noisy panel clutter, generic SaaS polish, unreadable dark-on-dark contrast, and mobile layouts that require careful pinching or sideways scanning. Avoid broad visual effects that make the surrounding BotBooru page feel hijacked or break the site's own header, sidebar, and comment surfaces.

## Design Principles

1. Premium restraint: use fewer, sharper visual moves so the profile feels crafted rather than decorated.
2. Amber and black first: make the creator color identity unmistakable while keeping the surface clean and premium.
3. Firefly teal as detail: use teal for focused accents, headings, bullets, and image details rather than the main brand color.
4. Readability is polish: copy-heavy sections need comfortable measure, contrast, and spacing on desktop and mobile.
5. Personality in the right places: keep the creator's direct voice, but let layout and hierarchy reduce friction.
6. BotBooru-safe by default: keep selectors scoped, respect the size budget, and treat page-level overrides as deliberate, documented exceptions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Prioritize readable contrast, comfortable body line height, mobile comfort, robust wrapping for buttons and links, and reduced-motion compatibility. No formal WCAG certification target is required, but readability should win when it conflicts with visual intensity.
