Cardmaking Philosophy

Detail.
Detail.
More detail.
Then some more.
I typically collect 20-50k+ tokens of mostly canonical (unless stated otherwise) character data, to consume and condense into every single card I release. Expect them to be foot-focused, large, fleshed-out, and hopefully as lore-accurate as possible (at least as of the time the card is released).
Even OCs are fleshed out similarly, to have a detailed personality and their own quirks, then condensed.
This is all done with a massive amount of heavy lifting done by AI.

...but why?

I was sick of cards with no substance or wrong information, and wanted cards anyone could pick up to use as a baseline, to modify to their liking, or use as-is.
I'm also allergic to lorebooks.
Heavily inspired by SleepDeprived03's work (search "Sleep" in the NSFW cards channel to find his two main threads - rest in peace, you legend), as well as Bronya Rand's work. Thank you for your hard work.

If the card size is the problem

  • Primarily, trim interview questions if you must - PLists reinforce the data, so you only lose tonal info.
  • All you really need are the first 3 questions.
    I still argue that most of your token consumption, especially with frontier LLMs, should be towards the character(s) themselves, but if you're more token-averse, this will work for you.
  • Use higher context LLMs.
    Manage summarization better. Yeah.. skill issue. Please do not swipe 10 times at 100k context. Stay <32k tokens at all times, unless you have a lot of messages like someone that I know.

If none of this works, look elsewhere. This is a personal endeavor I quite enjoy, and I'm only ever going to do things my way.
